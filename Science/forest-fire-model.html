Forest Fire
Model.

A small forest grows. Sparks arrive randomly. Most fires are small. A few become surprisingly large.

The spark is random.

The result is not only random.

It depends on the structure the spark encounters.

The model

At every step, a few empty cells become trees.

Then a spark lands randomly.

If the spark lands on empty space, nothing happens.

If it lands on a tree, the entire connected region burns.

Watch the distribution.

Small fires appear constantly.

Large fires appear rarely.

The large fire is not caused by a special spark. It happens when an ordinary spark reaches a large connected cluster.

The logic

Connections accumulate gradually.

Propagation appears suddenly.

The question is not only:

What started the fire?

But:

What made the forest ready to burn?
