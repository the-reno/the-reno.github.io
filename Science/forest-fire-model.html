Forest Fire
Model.

Trees grow. Sparks fall. Most fires are small. A few become large enough to reset the system.

The spark is random.

The outcome is shaped by the forest it lands in.

When trees are isolated, the fire dies. When trees form a connected cluster, the same spark travels.

The model

Empty spaces slowly grow trees.

Sparks arrive at random locations.

If a spark hits a tree, every connected tree burns.

The burned region becomes empty, and the process continues.

The logic

Connections accumulate gradually.

Propagation appears suddenly.
