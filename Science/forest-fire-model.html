The Spark

A forest fire model (Drossel-Schwabl)

The idea

Most sparks die.
A few change everything.

In nature, small events happen constantly. Most leave no trace.

A fire becomes possible only when enough trees accidentally connect, creating a path for flames to spread.

This model shows how many tiny events can produce mostly tiny fires and, occasionally, very large ones.

How it happens

4. Fire startsThe first tree burns.

Real life unfolds over minutes to years. The model compresses that timeline into thousands of small steps per frame.

What this means

Real forests are more complex: terrain, wind, moisture, species, fuel breaks, and human action all matter.

Still, this simple model captures one important truth:

When enough trees connect, a tiny spark can travel a long way.
