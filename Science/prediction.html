The Complexity
of Prediction.

In my view, prediction is one of the most interesting ideas in science. It starts with a dice, moves through Newton, then reaches places where even good equations stop giving one clean answer.

01 · dice

I used to think prediction was repetition.

I remember trying to throw a dice in exactly the same way.

Same hand. Same direction. Same strength.

Six. Then three. Then something else.

The movement looked almost identical. The result was not.

Maybe prediction was not about repeating the exact outcome. Maybe it was about understanding the range.
02 · probability

Randomness can still have structure.

A dice is simple: six sides, stable rules, known chances.

one dice
probability = successful possibilities ÷ all possibilities
example
probability of rolling 3 = 1 ÷ 6

With two dice, the pattern becomes visible. Seven is more common because more combinations create it.

03 · Newton

Then physics made prediction feel larger.

Newton. Gravity. The apple story.

What matters is not only the equation. It is the stability behind it.

stable value
gravity on Earth ≈ 9.81 meters per second²

Because gravity keeps acting, the apple gains speed every second.

velocity
velocity now = starting velocity − gravity × time

Position bends because speed is changing.

position
position now = starting position + starting velocity × time − ½ × gravity × time²
falling apple · height over time
heighttime →

The time² part is the signature of acceleration.

04 · approximation

Close enough can be powerful.

Science does not always need perfect precision. It needs useful precision.

A falling apple can be predicted without calculating every air molecule around it.

An equation is not reality itself. It is a compressed version of reality that works inside a useful range.

apple
Stable variables. Clear path.
dice
Unknown result. Known chances.
weather
Rules exist. Errors grow.
quantum
Probability is built into the model.
05 · quantum

At small scales, the idea changes.

For a long time, it felt natural to think: smaller object, same rules.

But at atomic scales, classical physics is not enough. The equation no longer gives one clean path. It gives probabilities.

quantum probability
probability of finding the particle = |wave function|²
classical

The object has a position. The equation tries to calculate it.

quantum

The equation gives possible outcomes. Probability becomes part of the theory.

06 · chaos

At large scales, prediction breaks again.

Weather. Traffic. Markets.

Not because the laws disappeared. Because interactions become enormous.

Two planets can be predictable for long periods. Add a third body and small errors grow.

three-body intuition
known rules + tiny errors + time = fragile prediction

The rules still work. Gravity still works. The equations still work.

Prediction is the part that breaks.

That is what makes it interesting.
