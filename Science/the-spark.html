critical systems

The
Spark.

Most sparks die. A few change everything. The difference is rarely the spark itself. It is the system surrounding it.

A spark can only travel as far as the system allows.

That is the idea.

Not every forest burns.

Not every idea spreads.

Not every technology becomes a standard.

The spark matters.

The structure matters more.

A simple model

Imagine a forest represented as a grid.

Each dot can be empty or contain a tree.

A spark lands in one place.

If the spark touches an isolated tree, the fire dies.

If it touches a connected cluster, the fire travels.

Move the density slowly.

At first, almost nothing changes.

Trees appear, but the fire remains local.

Then clusters begin to merge.

Eventually, the same spark reaches a much larger part of the grid.

The spark did not become stronger. The forest became more connected.

The logic behind it

What changes is not the event.

What changes is the number of available paths.

One tree becomes connected to another.

Small clusters become larger clusters.

Larger clusters become networks.

Eventually, a path exists.

Future Reach
=
Current Reach × (1 + Connectivity)

Current Reach

What the system has already touched.

Connectivity

The system's ability to carry an effect.

Future Reach

What becomes reachable next.

The equation is not a prediction.

It is a lens.

The next step depends on the structure that already exists.

Connections create the conditions for more connections.

The threshold

For a long time, nothing appears to happen.

Then one additional connection changes the behavior of the entire system.

In mathematics, this is often described as a critical threshold.

Below it, propagation remains local.

Above it, propagation becomes possible.

The system becomes capable of carrying the spark.

The pattern

Ideas.

Technologies.

Markets.

Cities.

Networks.

The pattern repeats.

Connections accumulate gradually.

Propagation appears all at once.

The spark may begin the process.

The network determines its reach.
